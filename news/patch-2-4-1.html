Patch 2.4.1 — Ashes of the Fallen Keep

The keep has fallen, the lines have shifted, and the balance of war has been redrawn. This patch delivers targeted class tuning, PvP adjustments, critical encounter fixes, and a significant performance pass across all servers.

March 12, 2026 Patch Balance PvP ~6 min read
Deployment Status

Patch 2.4.1 is live on all servers as of March 12, 2026 at 10:00 UTC. Server downtime lasted approximately 90 minutes. No character data was affected.

Overview

Patch 2.4.1 — designated internally as "Ashes of the Fallen Keep" — is a targeted maintenance release following the large Crimson Siege Chapter III expansion. Our primary goals with this patch were threefold: address the Shadowblade burst window that allowed players to delete targets before any meaningful counterplay was possible; deliver long-requested buffs to the Oathsinger, whose support role had fallen behind the current encounter tuning; and fix a critical exploit in the Pyremark Forge Colossus encounter that had been breaking the intended experience for thousands of players per week.

We have also shipped a significant GPU memory optimization that benefits all players on medium preset, and resolved the server crash that affected large-scale siege encounters with more than 500 simultaneous participants.

Class Balance Changes

All balance changes are the result of combined analysis from internal playtesting data, community feedback from the war council forums, and anonymous telemetry from the past 28 days of live play. Numbers reflect live values after patch.

Shadowblade Assassin
NerfExecute — Base damage reduced from 220% to 202% weapon power. Execute remains the highest single-target finisher in the game; this brings it in line with counterplay expectations.
BuffShadow Step — Cooldown reduced from 14s to 12s. This restores some mobility lost in the 2.3 pass without reverting the intent of that change.
BuffBleed — Maximum bleed stack cap increased from 5 to 6. Each additional stack now applies diminishing duration rather than full duration, rewarding sustained pressure.
FixDeath Shroud — Fixed a visual desync that caused opponents to see the Shadowblade's position incorrectly during stealth entry.
Oathsinger Support
BuffAegis — Duration increased from 6s to 8s. The barrier absorb value is unchanged. Support players requested this window increase repeatedly across multiple feedback cycles.
BuffResurrect — Mana cost reduced by 30%. Cast time unchanged at 2.5s. This makes battlefield resurrection viable in sustained encounters rather than a desperation ability.
BuffHymn of the Fallen — Effective radius increased from 20m to 25m. This corrects an unintentional reduction that occurred during the 2.2 movement pass.
FixAegis — Fixed an issue where Aegis would silently fail to apply when cast on targets affected by Voidcaller's Entropy debuff.
Arcanist Mage
BuffFrost Nova — Base radius increased from 8m to 10m. Freeze duration unchanged at 2.5s for single targets.
NerfFirestorm — Base damage per tick reduced by 5%. Duration extended by 1 second. Total damage output is nearly identical; the intent is to reduce spike windows and reward sustained positioning.
BuffChain Lightning — Maximum bounce count increased from 4 to 5. Each successive bounce continues to lose 15% effectiveness.
Warden Tank
BuffIron Will (Passive) — Passive physical resistance increased by 3 percentage points at all gear tiers.
NerfShield Wall — Reflected melee damage reduced from 25% to 15%. This was identified as unintentionally punishing melee-heavy classes too severely in 1v1 bracket content.
Dreadknight Berserker
BuffBlood Rage — Ramp-up time reduced from 20s to 16s. The Dreadknight's power curve was reaching maximum too slowly in encounters with high mobility opponents.
BuffUndying — Activation threshold increased from 20% to 25% max HP. At low health percentages the ability now triggers more reliably against burst damage.
Ironclad Vanguard
BuffJuggernaut — Movement speed penalty during charge reduced from 35% to 25%. Ironclad players reported being unable to close on mobile targets in open-field PvP.
BuffWar Cry — Area of effect radius increased from 6m to 8m. Affects both the taunt and the damage bonus granted to nearby allies.
Voidcaller Summoner
NerfRift — Cooldown increased from 24s to 28s. Rift portals were enabling faction-level PvP strategies that bypassed intended geographic chokepoints.
FixDemonbind — Summoned demons now correctly display their health bars to allied players. Previously this information was only visible to the Voidcaller.
Ashwalker Ranger
NerfHunter's Eye — Detection radius reduced from 40m to 35m. The increased radius introduced in 2.3 created visibility disparities that disadvantaged non-ranger classes in fog zones.
FixMarksman Piercing — Arrow penetration no longer applies through full siege wall thickness. It continues to work as intended on partial barriers, gates, and standard terrain.

PvP & Territory Control

The following adjustments address structural issues in large-scale territory PvP that had been compounding since the Crimson Siege Chapter III content opened three additional contested zones simultaneously.

  • Siege windows extended from 30 minutes to 45 minutes per zone. Guild coordination feedback consistently identified the original 30-minute window as too narrow for meaningful back-and-forth engagement.
  • Flag capture time in Ashfeld adjusted from 15s to 20s per player present. This reduces the effectiveness of overwhelming-numbers zerging and rewards coordinated small-team defense.
  • Guild territory HP scaling has been normalized for guilds with fewer than 40 active members. Smaller guilds were facing disproportionately difficult defense conditions against full-roster opponents.
  • Respawn suppression timer in active siege zones reduced from 12s to 9s. The longer timer was identified as punitive rather than strategic in high-intensity encounters.
  • Faction balance bonus — Servers with a population imbalance exceeding 40:60 across all three factions will now apply a minor damage and XP bonus to the minority factions until balance is restored.
Designer Note — Territory Tuning

We want siege warfare to reward preparation, coordination, and tactical execution — not raw numbers or burst windows. The changes in this patch are the first step in a broader rebalancing pass that will continue in 2.5 with significant guild war system improvements.

World & Encounter Fixes

Pyremark — Forge Colossus

A well-documented grapple technique allowed players to pull the Forge Colossus off its patrol path, bypassing the environmental hazards that define Phase 2 of the encounter. This was unambiguously unintended. The encounter boundary has been reinforced and the Colossus's patrol path now correctly constrains to its intended zone. Additionally, the Phase 2 ability rotation timer was reset to its original tuning — a previous hotfix had accidentally accelerated it by 8%.

Veilhaven — Merchant Quarter Pathfinding

NPC merchant pathing in the Veilhaven Merchant Quarter district has been completely rerouted. The original pathing grid was generating navigation deadlocks during peak server population, causing stalls that visually appeared as NPC freezes. Players will notice merchants moving more fluidly and consistently.

Northern Moor Dungeon — Dreadmoors

Floating terrain geometry at the dungeon entrance has been corrected. Several edge cases allowed players to clip through the floor geometry near the second gate. The collision mesh has been rebuilt for this area.

Performance & Stability

  • GPU memory footprint reduced by approximately 12% on the medium graphics preset. Players with 4–6 GB VRAM should notice improved frame consistency in dense environments like Veilhaven and Ashfeld's central market.
  • Server stability fix for large-scale siege encounters — the specific crash condition triggering at 500+ simultaneous participants has been identified and patched. This was caused by a memory leak in the collision broadcast system introduced in 2.3.0.
  • Ashfeld map loading time reduced by approximately 18% for players on HDD storage. SSD players will see minimal change as load times were already within acceptable range.
  • Shadow rendering pipeline updated to reduce GPU stutter during rapid scene transitions in the Pyremark volcanic zones.
  • Network interpolation improved for players with connections above 150ms. High-latency players should notice reduced desync during fast-paced ability exchanges.

UI & Quality of Life

  • New compact damage numbers option added to Display Settings. When enabled, nearby damage indicators are grouped and averaged rather than rendering individually in dense combat.
  • Class ability icons updated across all 8 classes with improved visual clarity at 1080p and 1440p resolutions. Icons now have sharper borders and better contrast against dark backgrounds.
  • Guild roster now supports sorting by last active date, guild contribution rank, and class type.
  • The in-game map now correctly displays guild-controlled territories with updated border colours that distinguish contested zones from fully held zones.
  • Tooltip delay has been reduced from 800ms to 600ms globally. This can be further customized in Interface Settings.

Looking Ahead

Patch 2.5 is currently scheduled for late April 2026 and will introduce the full guild war system overhaul, two additional Pyremark sub-zones, and the next chapter of the Crimson Siege storyline. A preliminary preview will be published to the War Dispatches in early April. A full public PTR build will be available for community testing approximately two weeks before live deployment.
